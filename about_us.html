One of the oldest countries in Africa[1] and continuously inhabited regions in the world, Ethiopian civilization's emergence dates back over thousands of years. Due to migration and imperial expansion, it grew to include many primarily Afro-Asiatic-speaking communities, including Amhara, Oromos, Somalis, Tigray, Afars, Sidama, Gurage, Agaw and Harari, among others.
Ethiopia has been able to preserve its rich history. Today, visitors can reap the rewards of ancient Abyssinia’s fierce patriotism by visiting the country’s many religious and historic sites.
